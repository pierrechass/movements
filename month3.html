Technical Objectives — Month 3

By the end of week 12, on top of everything retained from Months 1–2:

  • Scorpion, 5 controlled reps per side
  • A continuous rock-to-shoulder roll, both directions, 5 reps
  • At least two self-composed flows linking 4+ patterns fluidly
  • Get-Up, multi-directional, both sides, no hands
  • Wall-supported Headstand Hold, 20–30s, long line
  • Wall Handstand Prep near-full extension hold, 15–20s
  • Single-leg balance on an unstable surface, 15s+ per side
  • A clear personal sense of which qualities (mobility, control, coordination) are still your weakest link

Reading the Result

There is no pass/fail here — this is a learning system, not a certification. Compare all four checkpoints side by side. Look specifically for: which qualities improved fastest (usually static holds and CARs range), which lagged (often balance and rolling, since they depend on nervous-system adaptation rather than just tissue capacity), and which skill, if any, you'd want another full month on before moving toward the branches below.

Beyond Week 12

This program deliberately stops at wall-supported inversion and slow, controlled ground flows. The following are the natural next branches of the skill tree — each has a direct prerequisite already built by this program.

Freestanding HandstandDirect continuation of Wall Handstand Prep + Hollow Body. Requires a dedicated balance-on-hands progression (wall-walk removal, spotted kick-ups) — typically 3–6 more months.
Dive Roll / Forward Roll variationsContinuation of Rolling Patterns once the shoulder roll is fully automatic and you want more dynamic, traveling rolls — best learned with brief in-person coaching for safety.
Hanging & Pull-Up ProgressionContinuation of the optional Hanging Preparation entry, once equipment (a bar) is consistently available — active hangs → scapular pulls → first pull-up.
Loaded Carries & MovNat Terrain WorkTaking Get-Up, crawling, and balance outdoors onto uneven natural terrain — logs, slopes, rocks — which is where MovNat's practice actually lives.
End-Range Strength (PAILs/RAILs)The next layer of Functional Range Conditioning beyond CARs — training active strength at the very end of a range (useful for Scorpion depth and squat depth specifically).
Cartwheel & Lateral InversionA different inversion axis than Headstand/Handstand, building on the same balance and wrist-loading foundation, adding a rotational/lateral element.

Educational Resources — Month 3

  • Animal Flow — "Flow" conceptOfficial Animal Flow content on building your own flows (not just isolated "Form Wave" positions) mirrors exactly what Week 11 asks you to do.
  • GMB Fitness / Ido Portal — handstand progression theoryEither source's writing on wall-supported handstand progressions will contextualize why this program stays wall-supported and what a safe next step (kick-to-handstand, spotted) looks like.
  • Somatic practice & meditative movementLook into Feldenkrais or Somatic Experiencing's framing of "slow movement as a way of listening to the body" — it's the same principle underlying this program's exploration sessions, from a different tradition.
  • Functional Range Conditioning — PAILs/RAILs primerA short introduction to end-range isometrics prepares you conceptually for the "Beyond Week 12" branch of the same name.
