Month 2 of 3 · Weeks 5–8

Locomotion & Transitions

Month 1 built shapes you can hold still. This month puts them in motion and starts connecting them to each other — Beast becomes Beast Crawl, the squat becomes Ape, Beast and Crab meet in Sit-Through. Inversion is introduced conservatively, wall-supported only.

Technical Objectives — Month 2

By the end of week 8, on top of everything retained from Month 1:

  • Beast Crawl 10 steps forward and backward, hips level
  • Traveling Ape, 8+ low hops covering ground
  • Crab Walk 10 steps without hip dip
  • Sit-Through, 3 clean reps per side, Beast → Crab → Beast
  • Get-Up (half-kneeling stage), both sides, no hand support
  • Wall-supported headstand-prep tuck, 15–20s
  • Fetal Rock / Log Roll → first shoulder-roll rocks (Stage 1)
  • Single-leg balance with eyes closed, 20s+ per side

Gate Criteria — Ready for Month 3

Say yes to at least 7 of these 9 before moving into Month 3's integration work (Scorpion, full flows, Headstand Hold, Wall Handstand). If you're below that, spend one extra week on Month 2's Week 7–8 content.

  • All Month 1 gate criteria still hold (squat, hollow, arch, beast, crab, shin box, balance, no pain)
  • Beast Crawl, 10 steps forward and backward, hips level
  • Traveling Ape, 8+ low hops
  • Crab Walk, 10 steps, no hip dip
  • Sit-Through, 3 clean reps per side both directions
  • Get-Up half-kneeling, both sides, no hands
  • Wall headstand tuck, 15–20s, weight mostly on hands/forearms
  • Single-leg balance eyes closed, 20s+ per side
  • No pain in wrists, ankles, or neck

Educational Resources — Month 2

  • MovNat — natural ground locomotion philosophyMovNat's public articles/videos on crawling and quadrupedal movement explain why contralateral crawling patterns (Beast Crawl) are trained as a distinct skill, not just "core work."
  • Judo ukemi (breakfall) — rolling mechanicsAny beginner ukemi tutorial explains the diagonal shoulder-to-hip rolling line used in this program's Rolling Patterns — useful visual reference for why the roll avoids the head/neck.
  • GMB Fitness — wrist preparation articlesGMB's wrist-prep content pairs well with this month's increased wrist loading (Beast Crawl, Ape, headstand prep) — read before Week 6.
  • Motor learning — stages of skill acquisitionLook up Fitts & Posner's three stages (cognitive, associative, autonomous). Beast Crawl and Ape will feel like the "cognitive" stage this month — clumsy and effortful — knowing that's an expected phase, not a sign of doing it wrong, helps a lot.
