Month 1 of 3 · Weeks 1–4

Foundations

The goal of this month is not to be impressive. It is to install the raw materials — breath, active range of motion, two trunk shapes, four ground positions — that every later skill will be assembled from. Everything here is deliberately conservative.

Technical Objectives — Month 1

By the end of week 4 you should be able to do the following, calmly and with normal breathing — not as a strained max effort:

  • Hold an unassisted deep squat for 60s+, heels down
  • Hold Hollow Body and Arch Body for 30s each, extended limbs
  • Hold Beast and Crab statically for 20–30s, level hips
  • Complete 3–5 Shin Box switches per side without hands
  • Run the full CARs sequence (neck, shoulder, hip, spine) from memory
  • Single-leg balance 30–45s per side, eyes open
  • Tolerate 60s+ of accumulated flat-hand weight-bearing (wrist)
  • Perform 5 smooth Fetal Rocks and 3 Log Rolls each direction

Session Template (~40 minutes)

Every session in this program follows this same eight-part shape. What changes week to week is the content of each part, not the structure — this consistency is deliberate, so your attention goes to the movement rather than to remembering the format.

OrderComponentTimePurpose
1Joint Preparation (CARs)5 minActively map and open available range before loading it
2Breathing Practice3 minDownshift, establish the breath pattern used under tension
3Core & Trunk Control6 minInstall the trunk shape everything else borrows from
4Active Mobility7 minDeep Squat / Shin Box / wrist & ankle conditioning
5Ground Locomotion9 minBeast, Crab, Ape and their progressions
6Balance4 minProgressive proprioceptive challenge
7Guided Exploration4 minUnstructured movement using only what's been trained
8Cool-down3 minDown-regulate, brief body scan

Training 2–3×/week: treat the three variants below (A/B/C) as a rotating cycle — repeat A→B→C→A… regardless of calendar week, and simply move to the next week's table whenever you complete a full rotation. If training 4×/week, extend components 5–7 by a few minutes each to fill 45–60 min.

End-of-Month Assessment (repeat the Week 0 battery)

Run the identical baseline battery from the top of this document. Record the same eight numbers in your journal directly beneath the Week 0 entry so they sit side by side. You are looking for direction of change, not perfection — a Hollow Body hold going from 12s to 24s matters more than hitting any specific number.

Gate Criteria — Ready for Month 2

You do not need every number to be perfect, but you should be able to say yes to at least 6 of these 8 before moving on. If you're below that, repeat Week 3–4 for one more week rather than pushing into Month 2 on a shaky base — the locomotion and transition work there loads these exact qualities.

  • Deep Squat unassisted, heels down, 45s+
  • Hollow Body extended, 25s+
  • Arch Body extended, 25s+
  • Beast static hold, 20s+, level hips
  • Crab static hold, 20s+, level hips
  • Shin Box switch, both directions, hands-free
  • Single-leg balance eyes-open, 30s+ per side
  • No pain in wrists or ankles during any of the above

Educational Resources — Month 1

Optional but recommended. These build the conceptual map underneath the physical practice — useful for an analytically-minded learner.

  • Functional Range Conditioning — the concept of CARsSearch "FRC CARs" (Dr. Andreo Spina). Read about the difference between active and passive range of motion before Week 1 — it reframes every joint-prep drill in this program from "stretching" to "training."
  • Ido Portal — "Movement Culture" philosophy interviews/talksAny long-form interview (podcast or YouTube). Listen for how he frames floor work and the deep squat as baseline human positions, not warm-ups.
  • Animal Flow — instructional basics for Beast & CrabOfficial Animal Flow instructional content on the "Form Wave" (Beast/Crab/Ape/Scorpion family) gives useful visual reference for hip/shoulder alignment in these holds.
  • Proprioception & interoception — a short primerAny introductory article on proprioception (the sense of joint position/movement) and interoception (the sense of internal bodily state). This program trains both, and naming them helps you notice what you're actually sensing during a hold.
